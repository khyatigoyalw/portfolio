01 — Health & Wellness · D2C

Naturally Good

Brand Marketing Head · 2025 → Present

Took a wellness D2C brand from a quiet 2K audience to a 25K+ community with a real growth engine underneath — reporting straight to the founder, owning brand and performance as one.

12×Audience growth
4.2×ROAS · ₹20L/mo
-30%CAC
15K+App downloads
◆ The brand

Naturally Good is a health & wellness D2C brand — organic vegetables, honey, ghee, superfoods, seeds, teas and nutrition packs, backed by a farmer-first supply story. Great product, quietly under-marketed.

◆ The challenge

A tiny 2K audience, brand awareness that didn't match product quality, rising acquisition costs, and no clear reason for a shopper to choose it over Country Delight, Two Brothers or the quick-commerce shelf.

◆ My role

Brand Marketing Head, reporting to the founder. End-to-end owner: strategy, brand, content, performance, influencer, CRM, app growth and offline — the whole stack.

◆ Objectives
  • Reposition the brand around a memorable, ownable story
  • Grow an engaged organic community, not a vanity following
  • Build a profitable performance engine at a defensible CAC
  • Launch and scale the app as a retention & subscription channel
◆ Strategy

I repositioned Naturally Good from "another organic store" to a farm-to-family wellness ritual — anchoring every message in the farmer, the source and the health outcome. Then I built a content-and-influencer flywheel on top, and put a disciplined performance layer underneath so paid, organic and retention pulled in the same direction.

◆ Execution
  • Organic & social: repositioned Instagram strategy, monthly content calendars, reels & story series, recipe and festival campaigns, healthy-lifestyle series, community games
  • Farmer storytelling: turned the supply chain into the brand's emotional core — farm events, apartment & lift activations, hyperlocal offline branding
  • Performance: Meta & Google Ads on ₹20L+/month, creative & funnel testing to cut CAC ~30% at 4.2× ROAS
  • Influencer: 50+ partnerships across 8 always-on channels, from nano food creators to wellness voices
  • Commerce & retention: Shopify D2C, Amazon listings & A+ content, quick-commerce presence (Zepto / Blinkit / BigBasket), WhatsApp & email flows, a subscription model and referral loops
  • App: launch strategy, download campaigns, Play Store optimization and referral drives to 15K+ installs with 22% Day-7 retention
  • Packaging & decks: packaging redesign and copy, plus marketing, sales & investor decks for the founder's office
◆ Channels
InstagramMeta AdsGoogle AdsShopifyAmazonZeptoBlinkitBigBasketWhatsAppEmailInfluencerSEO / BlogsAppOffline / BTL
◆ Lessons

The story did the heavy lifting. Once the farmer-first narrative was clear, every channel got cheaper — creative resonated, influencers had something real to say, and retention followed because people bought into a belief, not a discount.

02 — FMCG · Food · 0→1 Launch

CUPEESE

Founder's-office Brand Builder · Premium Cheesecakes

A cheesecake brand built from a blank page — name, identity, packaging, positioning and go-to-market — then launched to ₹8L+ in its first 90 days.

₹8L+First 90 days
5,000+Community
60%Repeat purchase
0→1From scratch
◆ The idea

A premium, single-serve cheesecake worth talking about. No brand, no audience, no shelf — just a recipe and the conviction that dessert could be a small, repeatable ritual people fall in love with.

◆ Naming & identity

I named it CUPEESE — cup + please, a little joyful, easy to say and own — and built the identity, logo and voice around indulgence you don't have to justify.

◆ Positioning

Not "cheapest cake nearby" but the small luxury you gift yourself. Premium feel, honest ingredients, and packaging built to travel and to photograph.

◆ Customer persona

Young urban indulgers and gifters — people who screenshot dessert, order for a friend's win, and repeat-buy when a brand makes them feel something. I designed the entire funnel around that emotional trigger.

◆ Launch & growth
  • Packaging & product: designed the box, label copy and the unboxing moment as the first marketing asset
  • Social & community: built a 5,000+ community from zero with craveable content, story series and community campaigns
  • Performance: hyper-local paid campaigns tuned to delivery radius and repeat windows
  • Commerce: food-delivery aggregators plus WhatsApp commerce for direct, high-margin orders
  • Retention & CRM: loyalty and WhatsApp flows that drove 60% repeat purchase — the number I'm proudest of
◆ Channels
InstagramMeta AdsWhatsApp CommerceFood AggregatorsCommunityCRM / LoyaltyPackaging
◆ Lessons

In food, repeat is the whole game. Getting the first order is marketing; getting the second is the brand. Packaging, community and CRM did more for LTV than any single ad ever did.

A food brand leaning too hard on paid, with thin organic visibility and rising costs. The job: build durable, compounding demand that didn't reset to zero the moment ad spend paused.

◆ Strategy

Treat SEO and content as a flywheel, not a checkbox. Own the searches customers actually make, build community and CRM to keep them, and let organic quietly take load off the paid budget.

◆ My role

Brand & marketing strategy across the food business — SEO, content, community, CRM and budget across social, WhatsApp commerce and food-aggregator channels.

◆ Execution

04 — Growth · Funnel · Lead Gen

Exotic Learning

Talent & Growth · Recruitment Funnel

Where it started: a growth-and-conversion funnel that turned 1,000+ leads into 90 placements — proof that the fundamentals of demand are the same, whatever you're selling.

1,000+Leads
90Placements
10K+Pipeline
-20%Time-to-fill
◆ The challenge

Fill 15+ roles fast, from a cold start, with no established pipeline — and do it efficiently enough that the funnel could scale.

◆ Approach

I ran recruitment like a growth campaign: sourced and nurtured candidates as an audience, built conversion into every stage, and optimized the funnel on real numbers.

◆ Result

1,000+ leads into 90 placements (9% conversion) across 15+ roles in three months, a 10K+ candidate pipeline, and time-to-fill cut ~20%.

◆ Why it matters

Inside my marketing brain

The thinking
before the tactics.

Anyone can run ads. The difference is how you think about the brand before you spend a rupee. Here's the operating system behind the work.

◆ First contact

Questions I ask a new brand

  • Who exactly is this for, and who is it not for?
  • What truth can we own that competitors can't copy?
  • What job are people really hiring this product to do?
  • What does one loyal customer earn us over a year?
  • If we vanished, what would our people miss?
◆ Positioning

Brand archetypes I design around

I pick the archetype the brand can own with conviction, then make every asset ladder up to it.

CaregiverEverymanCreatorSageExplorerLoverHero
◆ Acquisition

The growth funnel

  • Awareness — story-led reach that earns attention
  • Consideration — proof, reviews, creators, content
  • Conversion — offer, page and checkout that close
  • Retention — CRM, subscription, community
  • Referral — make advocacy the cheapest channel
◆ Compounding

The content flywheel

Content earns attention → attention becomes community → community creates proof → proof lowers CAC → cheaper growth funds more content. Every turn makes the next cheaper.

◆ Psychology

Consumer levers I pull

  • Identity — "people like me buy this"
  • Ritual — build the brand into a habit, not a purchase
  • Social proof — creators, community, UGC
  • Loss & scarcity — used honestly, never cheaply
◆ Measurement

Numbers I refuse to fake

Vanity metrics feel good and pay nothing. I run on CAC, ROAS, retention curves, repeat rate and LTV:CAC — because those are the numbers a founder actually raises and hires against.
