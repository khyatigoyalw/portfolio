Building D2C brands in India

What actually works when quick-commerce, CAC and a genuinely offline-first customer reshape the funnel — lessons from running lift media, society activations and lifecycle journeys on the ground.

The funnel doesn’t start online here

A lot of D2C playbooks assume the customer's first touchpoint is a scroll-stopping ad. For a subscription organic brand like Naturally Good, that assumption breaks fast — the target customer is a health-conscious family in a premium residential society, and digital ads alone weren't creating the local presence or trust that a recurring farm-to-home subscription needs. The insight that changed the strategy: our ideal customers spend a few uninterrupted seconds in an apartment lift every single day, and that's a moment worth owning.

Hyperlocal beats broad, every time

I planned and executed a lift media campaign across premium societies in Gurgaon, eventually overseeing the installation of more than 500 lift posters. The objective wasn't reach for its own sake — it was consistent, repeated exposure within a tightly defined neighbourhood, so residents saw the brand daily until it stopped feeling like advertising and started feeling like a fact about the building they lived in.

Stack the channels, don’t rotate them

Lift posters alone aren't a strategy — they're one layer. I combined lift media with apartment activations and door-to-door sampling inside the same societies, building a connected 360° journey instead of three disconnected tactics: awareness in the lift, a tactile product experience at the activation, and a direct conversation with a sampling team at the door. Each layer made the next one convert better, because the resident had already half-recognised the brand before the human conversation even started.

Different customers need different lifecycles

Running lifecycle marketing across two very different businesses — HIOC, a café where decisions are impulsive, and Naturally Good, a subscription that needs trust and reassurance before a customer commits — taught me lifecycle marketing is never one-size-fits-all. A café customer needs a great first visit and a reason to come back this week. A subscription customer needs education, proof, and a slow build of confidence before the first order, let alone the fifth.

You can’t manage what you can’t see in one place

With activity spread across Meta, Google, WhatsApp, offline activations, Amazon and a sales team, no single platform's report could answer basic questions like which channel actually drove the best leads. Building centralized growth dashboards across three brands was what made offline and online marketing comparable on the same terms, and what let us reallocate budget toward what was actually working instead of what was easiest to measure.

D2C in India rewards brands that treat offline and online as one connected system, not two separate budgets. The lift poster, the sampling table and the retention email all have to tell the same story, in the order the customer actually experiences them.
